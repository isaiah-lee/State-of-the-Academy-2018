                Sexual orientation is "a person's sexual identity in relation to the gender to which they are attracted" (Oxford Dictionaries). Each term is defined as follows:

                • Asexual — without sexual attraction
                • Bisexual — sexually attracted to both men and women
                • Demisexual — sexually attracted only after developing an emotional connection
                • Heterosexual — sexually attracted to the opposite sex or gender

                • Pansexual — sexually attracted to others, regardless of sex or gender

                • Heteroromantic — romantically attracted to the opposite sex or gender

                School

                Academics and coursework are a large part of the life of an Andover student, comprising both the school day and an average of 4 hours outside of the classroom, with 115 students spending 6 hours outside of the classroom. On top of coursework, many students also participate in additional extracurricular activities. Nearly 64% of respondents reported that part of the reason they participate in extracurricular activities is to increase the chances of admission to a selective college. When looking at college admissions, Andover students are nearly split as to whether attending Andover would increase their chances of admission to a selective college, with 50.9% reporting that it would help their chances positively and 42.3% reporting that it would negatively impact their chances.

                Wellness

                Addressing student wellness has been at the forefront of Andover’s mission to help students stay balanced. 78.65% of students generally consider themselves happy at Andover, but only 63.09% of students think Andover students are happy. The largest percentage of students (38.52%) find their support system in their friends, with support systems including faculty and Sykes staff in addition to friends coming in a close second (33.40%). Less than half of the student body has visited Graham House or Sykes Wellness Center to see a counselor. On campus, 166 students have been diagnosed with depression and 187 students have been diagnosed with anxiety.

                Discipline

                When it is determined that an Andover student has committed a major offense, they are called upon a Disciplinary Committee (DC) that is usually comprised of their cluster dean, DC representative, cluster co-president, faculty DC representative, and house counselor or day student advisor. If a response to the student’s offense is deemed reputable, there are nine possibilities: Work Hours, Disciplinary Restriction, Bounding, Dean’s Reprimand, Censure, Warning, Probation, Suspension, and Dismissal. 78.04% of students believe that the school’s disciplinary system favors students of privileged backgrounds. Of all socio-economic classes, the highest percentage of students who have ever sat before a DC was lower class (18.18%), but the highest number of students who have ever sat before a DC was of the Upper class (24 students, 7.89% of Upper class respondents).
